**Engaging a qualified Conveyancer or Solicitor.**

Here's why and what that means for each party:

1.  **For the Seller:**
    - **What:** Before you can even list your property for sale, your conveyancer/solicitor will prepare the **Contract of Sale** and the accompanying **Vendor's Statement** (also known as a Section 32 Statement in Victoria, Prescribed Documents in NSW, etc.). This package contains all the legally required information about the property that potential buyers need to see.
    - **Why:** It's illegal to advertise a property for sale without having these documents prepared and available. Your conveyancer ensures your property is legally ready to be marketed.

2.  **For the Buyer:**
    - **What:** Once you've found a property you're interested in, your conveyancer/solicitor will **review the Contract of Sale and Vendor's Statement/Prescribed Documents** _before you sign anything_.
    - **Why:** This review is absolutely critical. They will identify any potential red flags, onerous conditions, issues with easements, covenants, zoning, or other matters that could affect your ownership or use of the property. They can also advise on any special conditions you might want to include (e.g., subject to finance, building and pest inspection).
    - **Crucial Advice:** Never sign a Contract of Sale for a property without having it thoroughly reviewed by your conveyancer or solicitor first, even if it's "subject to" various conditions.

**In summary, the first _legal_ step in conveyancing is to get your chosen conveyancer or solicitor involved early in the process to prepare or review the essential legal documentation.** This proactive approach protects your interests, whether you're buying or selling.%
